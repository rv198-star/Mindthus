## Purpose And Boundary

This packet tests the boundary introduced by the proposed V0.6 N+1 exclusion:
a request to turn a temporary, case-bound workaround into a standing default
for future cases is not another local repair merely because three prior
workarounds and urgency are present.

The packet has ten cases: three permanent-baseline negatives, two ordinary
normal-path positives, two bounded-emergency positives, and three neighbor
negatives. It is a new source packet; it neither changes nor pools with
`docs/benchmarks/brake-semantic-triage-non-shadow-calibration-texts-v0.2.md`,
`tests/brake_semantic_triage_v02_calibration_cases.jsonl`, or the stopped V0.2
run. N08/N09 are burned evidence, not templates, and N09--N14 remain
unassessed evidence outside every denominator.
