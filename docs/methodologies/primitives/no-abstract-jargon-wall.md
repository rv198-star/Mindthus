# No Abstract Jargon Wall / 先说人话

## Role

No Abstract Jargon Wall keeps Mindthus outputs understandable. It asks the agent to
name its stance, served reader, and intended effect before using method vocabulary.

It is not style polish. The core move is expression positioning: know whose position
the answer speaks from, who the words directly serve, and what understanding,
judgment, action, or risk awareness should change.

## Short Rule

先做表达定位：我代表什么立场、文字直接服务谁、要把对方带到哪里；先用例子、类比或
直接后果讲清楚，再使用 Mindthus 术语。

## When It Intervenes

- Before public-facing route answers.
- Before freeze, handoff, or user-facing summaries.
- When method names or abstract labels are starting to replace meaning.

## Action Effects

- Rewrite in plain language.
- Name the reader and decision/action being served.
- Move method names after the concrete meaning.
- Remove terms that do not change understanding.

## Must Not

- It must not become style polish detached from judgment.
- It must not delete necessary precision.
- It must not hide uncertainty just to sound simple.

## Probe

Before method labels, ask: what position am I speaking from, who is this for, and what
should they understand or do differently after reading it?
