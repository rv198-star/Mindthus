# MPG Pressure Tests

These pressure tests show whether MPG / Mainline-Path Game creates a Path-Carrying
Strategy instead of a generic risk/opportunity paragraph.

## Scenario 1: AI Stock Carrier Drawdown

Input:

> AI will definitely work long-term. Should I hold a concentrated AI equity position?

Expected baseline failure:

- repeats the mainline
- says “long-term bullish, short-term volatile”
- gives a generic risk warning without changing the carrier

Expected treatment behavior:

- qualify the mainline by horizon, actor, and vehicle
- separate AI demand from the current stock or portfolio carrier
- map valuation, rates, regulation, capex, competition, and narrative drawdown
- ask whether the carrier can survive one or more 70% drawdowns
- output a Path-Carrying Strategy with staged exposure, liquidity reserve, rebalancing
  triggers, and a `mainline_challenge` if the vehicle cannot survive

## Scenario 2: Central-Local Governance

Input:

> The central government wants long-term deleveraging, but local governments keep
> supporting land finance and housing.

Expected baseline failure:

- treats local resistance as stupidity or bad execution
- says central direction will eventually win
- ignores local fiscal survival pressure

Expected treatment behavior:

- qualify the mainline by policy horizon and implementation mechanism
- map local fiscal revenue, employment, stability, debt rollover, and cadre incentives
- distinguish central direction from local carrier and execution vehicle
- set trigger conditions for when local easing is path volatility versus mainline
  downgrade
- output a Path-Carrying Strategy that respects central-local game volatility

## Scenario 3: Company Transformation Cashflow

Input:

> The company must transform from old services to an AI-native subscription model.

Expected baseline failure:

- says transformation is necessary
- recommends full commitment because the future is clear
- ignores cashflow valley and sales incentive mismatch

Expected treatment behavior:

- qualify the transformation mainline by timeframe, customer segment, and revenue mix
- map old revenue dependence, sales compensation, customer migration, morale, and
  delivery risk
- separate the transformation mainline from the first implementation vehicle
- define exposure budget in cash runway, churn, team trust, and delivery capacity
- output a Path-Carrying Strategy with dual-track KPI, staged migration, probes, and
  exit triggers for the first vehicle

## Scenario 4: Technology Migration Carrier Risk

Input:

> We should move the platform to the new stack because it is obviously more efficient.

Expected baseline failure:

- repeats the new-stack efficiency argument
- ignores legacy integration, uptime, compliance, and team skill
- frames all resistance as conservatism

Expected treatment behavior:

- qualify the mainline as a migration claim, not a universal tech slogan
- map legacy dependencies, team capability, compliance, uptime, migration sequencing,
  and rollback cost
- separate new-stack direction from current migration vehicle
- output a Path-Carrying Strategy with reversible slices, probes, fallback paths, and
  trigger conditions for scaling or pausing

## Scenario 5: Naked Mainline Skips MPG

Input:

> People eventually die, so this person will die.

Expected baseline failure:

- treats a true universal statement as an actionable forecast
- starts path analysis without a constrained claim

Expected treatment behavior:

- reject the naked mainline as insufficient for MPG
- require a constrained mainline such as “within 10 years under specific medical,
  age, or exposure conditions”
- return to evidence gathering, SELA, or EDSP if the constrained claim cannot be formed

## Scenario 6: Missing Evidence Blocks MPG

Input:

> This founder will become a billionaire. How should we carry the path?

Expected baseline failure:

- invents personality traits or market facts
- creates an impressive strategy from missing evidence

Expected treatment behavior:

- identify missing evidence about capability, market, ownership, runway, and prior
  execution
- apply Evidence / Claim Ceiling before MPG
- produce no strong Path-Carrying Strategy until the factual ceiling is raised

## Scenario 7: Pure Control Boundary Routes To WAE

Input:

> Should the release gate be a deterministic script, agent review, or human approval?

Expected baseline failure:

- forces a mainline narrative about automation
- creates unnecessary path strategy

Expected treatment behavior:

- route to WAE because the active object is control boundary
- use MPG only later if a qualified automation mainline must be carried through
  adoption volatility and trust constraints

## Scenario 8: Constrained Mainline Challenge

Input:

> Housing is long-term pressured, so a highly leveraged short position should work in
> the next 12 months.

Expected baseline failure:

- accepts the long-term mainline as immediate trade support
- ignores policy rebound and margin-call path risk

Expected treatment behavior:

- qualify the constrained mainline as “housing declines enough within 12 months before
  the leveraged carrier is forced out”
- map credit easing, local fiscal rescue, sentiment rebounds, liquidity, and margin
  pressure
- separate structural housing pressure from the leveraged short vehicle
- output a Path-Carrying Strategy that may accept long-term pressure but reject or
  downgrade the constrained mainline for this carrier

## Scenario 9: War Of Resistance Time-Slice

Input:

> 抗日战争中国必胜，但请回到毛泽东发表《论持久战》之前，隔离你知道的未来信息，
> 只凭当时知道的信息推演。

Expected baseline failure:

- uses later victory as hidden proof
- mentions future information such as Pearl Harbor, U.S. entry, Soviet entry, or atomic
  bombs
- starts with `qualified_mainline`, `carrier_vehicle`, or other internal fields
- gives a correct-looking but unreadable worksheet

Expected treatment behavior:

- explicitly exclude future information
- give a plain-language conclusion first
- use a sentence like: “在当时信息下，不能把中国必胜当成已知事实；更稳的判断是，
  日本很强，但很难快速吞下中国，只要中国不投降、不崩盘，把战争拖长，中国胜算会
  随时间上升。”
- explain in ordinary language that Japan's short-war path was strong, but its long-war
  occupation path was hard to carry
- only then map the Path-Carrying Strategy: strategic depth, continued resistance,
  national mobilization, guerrilla and regular-war combination, morale preservation,
  and triggers that would downgrade the mainline

## Scenario 10: Stock Playback Reasoning Durability

Input:

> Pick a stock, cut the information at a past date, make an MPG judgment, then check the
> later price and events.

Expected baseline failure:

- scores the run by whether the stock later went up or down
- treats later outcome as proof that the original reasoning was right or wrong
- ignores that the original information may have been incomplete
- fails to separate reasoning quality from result playback

Expected treatment behavior:

- state that later outcome is not the score
- evaluate the original run under the information available at the time
- check whether the reasoning separated mainline from carrier, named path risks, and
  kept evidence boundaries honest
- treat information gaps honestly: 结果不准不必然失败 when the missing fact was not
  available in the slice
- use later events only to test whether trigger conditions worked
- require that trigger conditions should explain when a later result changed the
  judgment
